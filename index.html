LONG ISLAND ICE TEA

Long Island Ice Tea is a popular cocktail based on vodka, gin, tequila and rum. In addition, the cocktail usually includes triple sec (or Cointreau), cola (or iced tea) and so on. "Long Island" is one of the strongest cocktails (contains approximately 28 volume percent alcohol). Classified as a long drink. It is among the official cocktails of the International Bartenders Association (IBA), the category "Modern classics".

OLD FASHION

Old Fashion - cocktail aperitif of the International Association of Bartenders. It is mixed in a glass based on bourbon, scotch or rye whiskey. As a side dish, it is decorated with a slice of orange and a cocktail cherry. It is among the official cocktails of the International Bartenders Association (IBA), the category "Unforgettable".

NEGRONI

Negroni is an Italian cocktail made from one part gin, one part rosso vermouth (red, semi-sweet) and one part campari, decorated with orange peel. This is considered an aperitif.

MARGARITA

Margarita is an alcoholic cocktail based on tequila with liqueur and lime juice. It is one of the official cocktails of the International Bartenders Association (IBA), the category "Modern classics", previously included in the category of aperitifs. By volume, it is classified as a long drink ("long cocktail"). One of the most consumed cocktails in the world, the most famous and popular of tequila-based cocktails, and perhaps one of the very first such cocktails. Because of the sour taste, it belongs to the sour cocktail group.

MOJITO

Mojito is a cocktail based on light rum and mint leaves. Originates from the island of Cuba, became popular in the USA in the 1980s. The cocktail is included in the list of "modern classics" of the International Bartenders Association (IBA) and is classified as a long drink. Mojito traditionally consists of four ingredients: sparkling water, sugar, lime and mint.
